Write down the Taylor polynomial of order 4 (i.e., up to the 4-rd power of x) at the point $x_0 = 4$ of the function.
$$f(x) = ln(x) + x^2$$

---

Check [[Taylor Polynomial]]

For the function f(x), the Taylor polynomial around $x_0$ is:
$$T_4(x) = f(4) + f'(4)(x-4) + \frac{f''(4)}{2!}(x-4)^2 + \frac{f'''(4)}{3!}(x-4)^3 + \frac{f^{(4)}(4)}{4!}(x-4)^4$$
So all we need are $f(4), f'(4), f''(4), f'''(4), f^{(4)}(4)$

Compute the derivatives:
$f'(x) = \frac{1}{x}+2x$
$f''(x) = \frac{1}{x^2}+2$
$f'''(x) = \frac{2}{x^3}$
$f^{(4)}(x) = \frac{6}{x^4}$
